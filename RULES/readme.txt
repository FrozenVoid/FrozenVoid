RULES.txt is a politics-free alternative to Code of Conduct,
 based on avoidance of logical fallacies and emotion-based reasoning.
It governs all contribution to all https://github.com/FrozenVoid/  repos.

Preamble:
This project(s) is built on the goodwill and cooperation of its contributors,
with rules that define the consensus mechanism(logical validity) to ensure goodwill and 
cooperation are preserved without introducing any undue burden on participants: rules.txt doesn't strive to avoid conflict, but
to make debates on issues more civilized and rational.

Rules:
1 - Address the argument, not the author.
1.No ad-hominem attacks:
 arguments must no rely on the qualities/characteristics of a person, only on their content.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ad_hominem
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poisoning_the_well
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_fallacy#Form

1b.Source of claim doesn't invalidate the argument itself.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_fallacy

1c.Popularity or authority doesn't imply validity.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argumentum_ad_populum
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argument_from_authority

1d.Natural/Moral value doesn't imply validity.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moralistic_fallacy
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naturalistic_fallacy

1e.Do not construct a fantasy narrative misrepresenting your opposition.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Straw_man


2 - Avoid emotional reasoning
2.Don't use emotional arguments in reasoning:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appeal_to_emotion

2b.Opinions have no relevance to argument presented:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I%27m_entitled_to_my_opinion
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thought-terminating_clich%C3%A9

2d.The consequences of a hypothesis don't imply its validity.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appeal_to_consequences

2e.Avoid generic loaded terms.
 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loaded_language



3 - Structure of discussion
3.Arguments cannot extend their initial claim scope to benefit the claim.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moving_the_goalposts

3b.Address the argument itself, not its specific application.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tu_quoque

3c.Don't derail discussion by inserting controversial off-topic content.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_herring

3d.Don't quote out of context.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quoting_out_of_context


3e.Don't assume a conclusion within a hypothethical scenario.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Begging_the_question



4 - Avoid generalizations

4.Avoid generalizing an example as equal to its category:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faulty_generalization

4b.Avoid systemic conclusions from analogy or example:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fallacies_of_illicit_transference
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argument_from_analogy

4c.Generalizations can't rely on specific exemptions.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No_true_Scotsman

4d.Don't assume there are only two valid positions:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/False_dilemma



5 - Don't assume simplicity where there is none

5a.Absence of something doesn't imply its non-existence:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argument_from_silence#Structure_of_the_argument

5b. Don't form conclusions out of thin air.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irrelevant_conclusion

5c.Logical structure of argument has to be valid for the conclusion to be valid,
regardless of truth value or appeal of premises.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formal_fallacy
